UX Research

During the research phase I familiarize myselve with the activity of Facebook groups related to the topic of zero/less waste. I concluded that the community mainly seeks knowledge about reusing things like unrepairable items, containers, trash, etc. We decided to give them a more structured plarform that would atract people interested in sustainibility.
Benefits of the app over current solutions: facebook groups
Facebook groups are known to be not structured too well and not the best for searching. With our product, we give users a better experience by getting more knowledgeable with upcycling possibilities. The community aspect of the app is encouraging and supportive, similar to facebook groups,

Product features

The main features of our web app allow users browse for ideas how to reuse an item (or items). As search results the app display easy, quick tips how to reuse items that does not require any modification of the items and DIY tutorials featuring those items. The algorithm of the apps filters results to display ideas that do not require using additional stuff that the user may not have. The idea of less waste is to limit consuption, so we don't want to encourage additional buying. The app is designed to be community-driven. Users can submit their responses on how to reuse a particular item, comment on the topic, rate the responses. The algorith than use their responses to give the best ideas for future search results

Difficulties and complications we encountered, and what we learned along the way

We spent quite a while on idealizing phase of the project. We would definetely benefit from using tools for evaluating and filtering ideas in a more agile way In the future we would definetely need additional front-end developer. The workload was quite a lot for two front-end members
